The built-in picker

macOS's own emoji panel (Control+Command+Space or the Globe key) is free, works in every app, and needs nothing installed. If you type emoji occasionally, it's probably enough. Its limits: no shortcodes, symbols only through the full Character Viewer panel (not a quick inline search), and no GIF support outside Messages.

Rocket

Rocket popularized inline colon-shortcode autocomplete on Mac — type :tada anywhere and it suggests 🎉 at your cursor, the same interaction Mojito uses. It's free to install; a $10 upgrade to Rocket Pro adds a dedicated search-and-browse window, a symbol catalog, GIF/image/meme storage, custom shortcuts, text expansion snippets, and usage stats. It's closed source.

Raycast

Raycast is a Spotlight-style launcher with an emoji picker built in — "Search Emojis and Symbols" is listed among its core, free features. The catch is where results land: Raycast surfaces them inside its own launcher window, not inline at your cursor in whatever app you're typing in. If you already have Raycast open constantly, that's barely a catch. Raycast Pro starts at $8/month billed annually ($10 month-to-month), but it's for AI models, cloud sync, and clipboard history — not required for the emoji picker. GIF search exists too, through a community extension rather than the core app.

Mojito

Mojito is free and AGPL open source. It autocompletes :shortcode: inline at your cursor in any app, matches by keyword (:happy finds 😀 even when you don't know the exact code), and adds two triggers the others don't have: :: for symbol search — math signs, currencies, Mac key glyphs — and ::: for Giphy-backed GIF search, with GIFs pasted as an actual file so they animate in Slack and Discord. Requires macOS 14 or later.

The honest verdict

If you already live in Raycast, its picker is probably enough — don't add a second tool for one feature. Rocket is polished, proven, and the app that put inline shortcodes on the map; $10 is fair for what Pro adds. Mojito's case is narrower: free, open source, and inline — symbol and GIF search that land at your cursor and cost nothing, where the others put them behind a Pro tier, a separate extension, or their own launcher window.

Picking between them

Is there a completely free emoji picker for Mac?

Yes — the built-in one, and Mojito. Rocket's free tier covers the core shortcode picker but reserves search/browse and GIF storage for the $10 Pro upgrade. Raycast's emoji picker is free; Raycast Pro is a separate subscription for unrelated features.

Why does an emoji app need accessibility permission?

Inline autocomplete has to read what you're typing and insert text into other apps — that's the macOS Accessibility API, and there's no way around it for this category of app. It's worth checking whether the app is open source before granting it, which is one reason we keep pointing at Mojito's source.

Can I keep using ⌃⌘Space too?

Yes, alongside any of these — the system shortcut and a third-party shortcode picker don't conflict. They're just two different ways to reach an emoji.
