How to type math symbols on a Mac (π, ∞, ±, ≈ and more)

Most of the common math symbols have an Option-key shortcut. A few surprisingly common ones — ×, superscripts, arrows — don't, and that's where things get annoying.

⌥option P → π

How do I type the pi symbol on a Mac?

Press Option + P and macOS types π. It works in any text field in any app, no setup needed. It's one of the few Greek letters with its own dedicated key combo — most of the alphabet has nothing assigned to it at all, which is covered further down.

The gaps macOS leaves open

A handful of everyday math symbols have no Option-key shortcut at all. The multiplication sign × is the biggest one — most people just type a lowercase x instead, which is why so much math in emails and chat looks like "3 x 4" rather than "3 × 4." The real character has to come from the Character Viewer.

Superscripts have the same problem. There's no key combo for ² or ³. If you're formatting a document in Pages or Word, the right tool is baseline-shift formatting on a regular "2," not a special character. If you need the actual Unicode superscript character — for a filename, a URL, plain text — that comes from the Character Viewer too.

Arrows are a third gap: → ← ↑ ↓ have no Option-key shortcuts either. Same fallback, same friction.

Greek letters beyond pi

π is the exception. λ, Δ, Ω, θ, and the rest of the Greek alphabet have no Option shortcuts on macOS — not one of them. Anyone who writes math, physics, or engineering notation regularly runs into this fast, since a single equation might need three or four Greek letters and macOS only gives you a shortcut for one.

Faster with Mojito

This is where Mojito earns its keep — everything above that has "Character Viewer only" as its native answer gets a shortcode instead. ::times for ×, ::rightarrow, ::leftarrow, ::uparrow, and ::downarrow for the arrows, ::lambda, ::Delta, ::omega, and ::theta for the Greek letters with no keyboard shortcut at all. Type the shortcode anywhere and it autocompletes at your cursor, so your eyes never leave the equation.

Greek letters, superscripts, and other follow-ups

How do I type Greek letters on a Mac?

Only π has an Option-key shortcut (Option + P). For everything else — λ, Δ, Ω, θ, and the rest — use the Character Viewer (⌃ + ⌘ + Space), search the letter's name, and double-click to insert. Mojito shortcuts this to a typed shortcode like ::lambda or ::theta.

Why does Option+V give me √?

Option+V is the built-in shortcut for the square root sign, not a checkmark. macOS doesn't have a checkmark shortcut at all — typing a real ✓ takes the Character Viewer or a shortcode.

How do I write x² on a Mac?

Depends on the tool. In Pages or Word, select the "2" and apply baseline-shift or superscript formatting from the Format menu — that's the correct approach for a document. If you need the literal Unicode superscript character in plain text, pull it from the Character Viewer (⌃ + ⌘ + Space, search "superscript two").
