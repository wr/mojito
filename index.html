    About

    Mojito lives in your menu bar and watches for a colon followed by a few letters. A picker appears at your cursor with fuzzy matches; use the arrows to choose and Return to insert. To skip the picker, type the closing colon — :heart: resolves directly. The same shortcodes you already use in Slack and GitHub now work in Notes, Messages, your terminal, anywhere you type.

    Emoticons work too. Type :) or <3 and they're translated as you go. Symbols use double colons: ::cmd:: for ⌘, ::option:: for ⌥, ::return:: for ↵. Mojito honors your default skin tone, and stays out of apps that already have their own emoji picker.

    Privacy

    Nothing leaves your Mac. Keystrokes are matched against the shortcode list locally; nothing is logged, transmitted, or stored. Password and other secure-text fields are ignored entirely. The only network request Mojito makes is checking for app updates.
